Projects

i-MADE stands for Illinois Medical Advancements through Design and Engineering. The student organization develops personalized and cost-effective medical technologies for local clients. I collaborated with other teammembers as a project engineer on the Smart Balance Board. By providing real-time feedback on patients' balance performance to physicians through LEDs lined around the edge of the board, the Smart Balance Board is able to maximize the training and rehabilitation of the ankle. Components included the Arduino Uno, gyroscope, accelerometer, and a wooden base model created with CAD. Based on the angle that the gyroscope measures, a certain number of LED pins will turn red indicating the direction that the user is leaning. In comparison to other bulky and inaccessible equipment, our Smart Balance Board easily returns information such as control stability to patients and physical therapists. Altogether, the Smart Balance Board team was awarded 1st place for an outstanding RSO Exhibit by the Engineering Open House judges.

Over the course of nearly two years, I collaborated alongside industry professionals to conduct a scientific study to determine the status of Vitamin D in children with the autoimmune disease PANS (also known as Pediatric Acute-onset Neuropsychiatric Syndrome). Not only did I learn skills such as meeting deadlines, but I also gained a lot of experience in Excel as one of my tasks included collecting data from a patient database and formatting that data in multiple Excel spreadsheets. The peak of my experience was when I had the opportunity to travel to Phoenix and present my research to a panel of scientists, doctors, and students at the American Academy of Allergy Asthma and Immunology meeting. The abstract of my research paper is now published in the Journal of Allergy and Clinical Immunology.
